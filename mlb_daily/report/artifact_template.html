How to Scan a Slate
Routine game: the two models agree on the winner, totals sit close to the market number, betting splits move together, starting pitchers are near their normal form, and there's no extreme weather/park effect. Nothing here needs a second look.

Notable game: one or more of the checks in the first section above trips. Notable doesn't mean "bet this" — it means there's more disagreement between sources than usual, which is exactly the kind of case worth comparing against the actual final score afterward.
Glossaryjargon used above, in plain English
ERA
Earned run average — average runs a pitcher allows per 9 innings. Lower is better.
xERA / xFIP
"Expected" versions of ERA that try to strip out luck/defense — same idea, lower is better.
K/9
Strikeouts per 9 innings, for a pitcher. Higher usually means more dominant stuff.
wRC+
A hitting stat where 100 = league-average offense. 120 means 20% better than average; 80 means 20% worse.
OPS
On-base plus slugging — a quick overall measure of a hitter's production. Higher is better.
ISO / K%
ISO = raw power (extra bases per at-bat). K% = how often a hitter strikes out — lower is generally better for the hitter.
BPP
MoundEdge's simulation-based projection — it runs the matchup many times and averages the outcomes, as opposed to a single-formula model.
Moneyline
A bet on which team simply wins the game, no point spread involved.
Total / Over-Under
A bet on whether the combined final score of both teams will be above or below a set number.
Bets% vs money%
% of individual bets placed on a side vs. % of total dollars wagered on it. When these two disagree a lot, it usually means a small number of large bettors are on the opposite side of the general public.
Home/road split
How much better or worse a pitcher performs at home vs. on the road, compared to their season average.
